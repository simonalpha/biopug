Bio Programming Users Group

University of Melbourne

Our goals

We aim to instill a solid understanding of basic programming techniques, and emphasise the importance of testing and reliability in scientific software.

In addition, we aim to create a community of coding researchers around the university, to provide opportunities for collaboration and support, as well as a forum for discussion around the use of programming in research.

Core Topics

  • Basic programming constructs
  • Thinking like a programmer
  • Testing
  • Version control
  • Using the shell

Other topics will be discussed given enough interest.

Can I get involved?

Definitely! We welcome everyone, regardless of programming experience or expected application domain. As a large number of our members are based in biological research, some exercises will involve problems from this domain. However, knowledge of biology is not a requirement, and should not impede your involvement.

While we are primarily using Python as our teaching language, we aim to be language-agnostic in the concepts we teach. Indeed, people with experience in other languages are encouraged to attend.
